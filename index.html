Our Story

Dirt Bikes USA was founded in 1996 by Carl Schmidt and Steven McFadden, two experienced bikers with engineering backgrounds who saw the growing popularity of dirt bikes in the United States. They developed custom frames optimized for off-road handling and began building their own models using top-quality motorcycle engines.

After Steven finished first in the famous Barstow to Las Vegas race on one of their customized bikes, demand exploded. They opened a production facility in Carbondale, Colorado, which has since grown to house over 140 employees.

Racing March 15, 2026

Dirt Bikes USA Takes Podium at Daytona Bike Week 2026

Our factory team secured two podium finishes at this year's Daytona Bike Week, showcasing the Enduro 550 and Moto 450 against the world's best.

The 2026 Daytona Bike Week was one for the record books for Dirt Bikes USA. Rider Alex Torres brought the Enduro 550 to a stunning second-place finish in the endurance category, while teammate Jordan Blake powered the Moto 450 to third in the motocross sprint event.

"This is a testament to the hard work of our entire team — from the engineers in Carbondale to the riders who push these bikes to the limit," said CEO Carl Schmidt. The results have driven a surge in pre-orders for the 2026 model year lineup.

Products January 20, 2026

Introducing the 2026 Model Year Lineup

All four models receive significant updates for 2026, including revised suspension geometry, updated ECU mapping, and new colorways.

The 2026 model year marks the biggest annual update in Dirt Bikes USA history. Every model in our lineup — the Enduro 250, Enduro 550, Moto 300, and Moto 450 — receives revised suspension geometry developed through extensive race testing at our Colorado proving grounds.

New ECU mapping provides smoother power delivery across all RPM ranges, and updated exhaust systems improve both performance and sound. Two new colorway options — Midnight Stealth and Desert Storm — join the classic Carbondale Orange.

Community February 8, 2026

Dirt Bikes USA Owners' Group Hits 5,000 Members

Our official owners' community has reached an incredible milestone, with riders from all 50 states connecting to share trails, tips, and adventures.

What started as a small forum in 2018 has grown into a vibrant community of over 5,000 Dirt Bikes USA owners. The group organizes monthly regional rides, shares maintenance tips, and connects riders of all experience levels.

To celebrate the milestone, we're hosting a National Owners' Rally at our Carbondale facility this summer, featuring factory tours, demo rides of the 2026 lineup, and a barbecue with the founding team. Stay tuned for registration details.

Tips April 1, 2026

Trail Riding Tips: Preparing for Spring Season

After a long winter, get your bike trail-ready with our comprehensive spring prep checklist from the Dirt Bikes USA service team.

Spring is the most exciting time for trail riders, but before you hit the dirt, your bike needs some attention. Our service team recommends starting with a full fluid check — engine oil, coolant, and brake fluid should all be fresh after winter storage.

Inspect your chain tension and lubrication, check tire pressure and tread depth, and test all electrical systems including lights and the kill switch. Don't forget to charge or replace your battery if it sat unused. A 30-minute pre-season inspection can prevent hours of trailside frustration.
